III-07 / Components Side Panel.

Side Panel covers the edge-anchored surfaces. The variant documented here is the drawer: a focus-trapping, scroll-locking modal pinned to a viewport edge instead of the centre — the edge-anchored counterpart to the centered modals on III-06. A future revision will add the non-blocking, in-flow side panel for supplementary context that stays anchored without a scrim.

Architectural note · the drawer is a modal

The drawer is the edge-anchored blocking surface. It traps focus, locks body scroll, and dismisses through the close button, backdrop, or Escape — the same dismiss contract as the centered modals on III-06. The only thing that differs is the surface anchor: instead of centring on the viewport, the drawer pins to one edge. A surface that does not do all three is a non-modal Side Panel, not a drawer.

01 / Drawer — Anatomy

Anatomy.

A drawer is a modal with its envelope re-anchored to a viewport edge instead of the centre. The interior — slot structure, eyebrow + title + description + action-row patterns, and the glass surface — is the standard Card pattern from III-05 and is identical to the centered modals on III-06. What makes this a drawer is the envelope: a full-height surface anchored to one edge, with the slide-in transform applied along the orthogonal axis on open.

The right edge is the convention for content reveals. Left-anchored drawers are reserved for navigation or workspace switchers. Sheets anchored to the bottom or top edge are not part of this page; promote those flows to the relevant component (Sidebar for navigation, Toast for system interrupts).

A drawer is still a modal. It traps focus, locks body scroll, and dismisses through the close button, backdrop, or Escape. A surface that does not do all three is a non-modal Side Panel, not a drawer.
