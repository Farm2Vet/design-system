III-18 / Components Chat input.

The transcript builder behind the admin conversation editor. A vertical stack of role-coloured turn cards — each a single chat message you can edit, reorder, and remove. Producer turns tint primary green; vet turns tint info blue, so the back-and-forth of a conversation reads at a glance.

Reorder a turn by dragging its grip handle, or with the per-card Up / Down buttons. The dashed Add entry row appends a new producer turn; the role control on each card cycles producer ↔ vet. This is an editor, not a renderer — for the read-only chat surface see V-02.

01 / Chat Input — Anatomy

Anatomy.

One turn card is a toolbar row over a message field. The toolbar carries — left to right — a drag handle, a role control, the zero-padded turn number, and a right-aligned action cluster (move up, move down, remove). Below the list sits the dashed add-entry row. Two roles tint the whole card.

A · turn card
.chat-input__turn is one chat message. The role modifier (--producer / --vet) re-points the --chat-turn-* trio so border, background, and accent text all tint together.
B · handle
.chat-input__handle is the grip. cursor: grab at rest, grabbing while held. Drag it vertically to reorder; touch-action: none keeps the pointer drag from scrolling the page.
C · role control
.chat-input__role pairs the role icon, role label, and a small chevron. Click cycles producer ↔ vet, re-tinting the card and swapping the icon + label.
D · turn number
.chat-input__turn-no renders the zero-padded, tabular mono index ("TURN 01"). It re-syncs after every reorder, add, and remove.
E · actions
.chat-input__actions is the margin-left:auto cluster: move up (disabled on the first card), move down (disabled on the last), and remove (--remove, hovers critical, disabled when only one card remains).
F · field
.chat-input__field is the message textarea — full width, resize-y, with the form focus ring.
G · add entry
.chat-input__add is the dashed full-width row below the list; it appends a producer turn and tints primary on hover.
02 / Chat Input — Roles

Roles.

Two roles, two colour families. Producer carries the user (farmer) voice and tints primary green; vet carries the clinician voice and tints info blue. The whole card tints — border, fill, and accent text — so a long transcript reads as alternating speakers without per-line labels.

The role tint is the only speaker signal — keep both roles in play. A transcript that's all one role reads as a monologue, not a conversation. The role control exists so an author can fix a mistyped speaker in place; don't repurpose it as a generic "category" toggle.

03 / Chat Input — States

States.

Rest, the lifted drag preview, the focused field, and the disabled action edges (first card can't move up, last can't move down, a lone card can't be removed). The dragging source dims to opacity: .4 while its clone floats.

04 / Chat Input — Live

Try it.

A working editor seeded with three turns. Drag a handle to reorder, use Up / Down, click a role control to flip the speaker, remove a turn, or add a new one. Turn numbers re-sync after every change.

Reorder by the handle or by Up / Down — never by drag on the field. The textarea owns text selection and the caret; hijacking pointer-down there for drag would steal the author's ability to edit. Drag lives on the grip alone.
