Management

Vietnam has commercialized African swine fever (ASF) vaccines (NAVET-ASFVAC, AVAC ASF LIVE). These are live attenuated vaccines.

Risk

Live attenuated vaccines carry risks of vaccine virus shedding into the environment or reversion to virulence if biosafety management and vaccination practices are inadequate.

Recommendations

  • Use only in fattening pigs. Extreme caution is required, or vaccination should be avoided, in breeding sows (unless the latest official guidance from the Department of Animal Health confirms safety for pregnancy), due to the risk of embryonic/fetal effects and vertical transmission.
  • Vaccinate pigs at 4–8 weeks of age.
  • Closely monitor post-vaccination reactions within the first 72 hours.

Treatment

No effective treatment exists; antibiotics and antipyretics are ineffective. Recommended actions include immediate reporting to veterinary authorities, culling of the herd, and implementing strict biosecurity and disinfection protocols.

Additional

In the event of suspected African Swine Fever (ASF), Classical Swine Fever (CSF), or other diseases for which treatment is prohibited, the following actions are mandatory:

1. Immediate disease control measures

  • Isolate all affected pigs immediately.
  • Halt all animal movement on and off the farm.
  • Do not treat further until the official veterinary authority arrives.
  • Monitor new cases closely for clinical signs (fever, off-feed/anorexia, lethargy, skin discoloration, diarrhea).

2. Official notification

  • In Vietnam, contact the Sub-Department of Animal Health (Chi cục Thú y) or the Sub-Department of Livestock and Animal Health (Chi cục Chăn nuôi và Thú y) in your area.
  • ASF is a mandatory reportable disease under Vietnamese animal health law.

3. Sample submission for testing

  • Samples should be collected when pigs show clinical signs (e.g. fever).
  • Do not perform full necropsies as this may spread contamination.

Sampling targets:

  1. Pigs suspected of ASF: off-feed, sick, fever >40°C, or dead.
  2. F1, F2 generation pigs from suspected animals (5–10 samples).
  3. Blood samples for ASF surveillance.

Note on testing:

  • Up to 5 samples may be pooled per RT-PCR test.
  • Send samples to at least two laboratories for cross-validation.

Do not

  • Sell, slaughter, or bury pigs without government authorization.
  • Disinfect the housing immediately — this can interfere with sample collection.

If ASF is confirmed

  • All pigs on the farm may be culled according to national policy.
  • The farm will be disinfected under official supervision.
  • Enhanced biosecurity and movement restrictions will be implemented.

Forms

Acute

Disease rates

Mortality

The mortality rate is very high, ranging from 0% to 100% depending on the virulence of the virus:

  • For highly virulent isolates, usually 90–100%.
  • For moderately virulent isolates, approximately 20–40% in adult pigs and 70–80% in young pigs.
  • For low virulence isolates, approximately 10–30%.

Peracute form — Pigs usually die rapidly within 1–4 days after the onset of clinical signs.

Acute form — the most common and typical form — Mortality is very high, occurring within 7 days.

Subacute form — Pigs may die (30–70% mortality) or recover after 3–4 weeks.

Morbidity

The morbidity rate ranges from 40% to 85%, depending on the virulence of the virus isolate and the route of infection. In acute outbreaks, the morbidity rate can reach 100%.

Fever

High fever 40–42°C.

Digestive
Reduced appetite Anorexia Vomiting Constipation Diarrhea Melena

Acute form is the most common and typical form. Reduced appetite or anorexia. Vomiting, constipation, or diarrhea (feces may contain blood — melena).

In chronic form, nonspecific clinical signs are observed, which may include emaciation (wasting), swollen joints (arthritis), and respiratory problems.

Rapid breathing (tachypnea), dyspnea, nasal discharge (may contain blood or foam) due to pulmonary edema.

Prevention

Currently, there is no treatment for this disease. In this case, the recommendations include:

Immediate disease control

  • Immediately isolate all affected pigs.
  • Stop all movement of pigs in and out of the farm.
  • Do not provide further treatment until intervention from the veterinary authorities.
  • Closely monitor for new cases with symptoms such as fever, loss of appetite, fatigue, skin discoloration, or diarrhea.

Notify local veterinary authorities

  • In Vietnam, contact the local Sub-Department of Animal Health or the Sub-Department of Livestock Production and Animal Health.
  • African Swine Fever is a notifiable disease under Vietnamese veterinary law.

Send samples for ASF testing

  • ASF testing should be conducted when pigs show clinical signs such as fever.
  • Collect inguinal lymph node samples; avoid performing necropsies to prevent environmental contamination with the virus.

What not to do

  • Do not sell, slaughter, or bury pigs without government permission.
  • Do not disinfect the farm immediately, as this may interfere with sample collection for testing.

Handling based on severity

  • Very severe outbreak: depopulate the entire herd to prevent the risk of spread. Clean and disinfect the entire farming area and its surroundings.
  • Severe outbreak, not yet herd-wide: cull problematic groups and high-risk individuals. Strictly control spread; handle empty pens and related areas. Restocking only with control and risk assessment.
  • Less severe cases: flexible handling — quickly cull sick or at-risk groups. Strict spread control and farm hygiene must still be maintained. Restocking only after biosecurity confirmed.

ASF prevention and control procedures

A comprehensive and strict ASF prevention and control procedure should be applied, including:

  • Human control: restrict farm access, implement disinfection, change protective clothing.
  • Insect and rodent control: eliminate intermediate hosts.
  • Tool and equipment control: ensure all tools are disinfected.
  • Pig flow control: strictly manage movement.
  • Handling and disposal of problematic pigs: culling, isolation, destruction, testing.
  • Farm hygiene: regular and thorough disinfection.
  • Controlled restocking: only after the facility meets biosecurity standards, with veterinary supervision.
