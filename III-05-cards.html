III-05 / Components Card.

A bounded container that groups related content. Composed from a slot-based anatomy and modified through style, layout, functional, and surface variants. All declarations resolve through the --card-* token layer.

Architectural note · scope boundary

FAQ / Q&A patterns are documented under Accordion, not Card. A disclosure that expands to reveal hidden content has its own keyboard contract, animation timing, and aria semantics. Card therefore does not ship a .card--question variant; the equivalent affordance lives under component-accordion.html.

Cards are glanceable surfaces. A card surfaces the most important fact first and pushes detail to a destination. If a reader has to study a card to extract its meaning, the card is doing too much.

01 / Card — Variants

Elevation.

Modifiers that control the card's surface treatment. Elevated: shadow plus border (default). Filled: tinted background, no shadow. Outlined: thicker border, no shadow. Left-bar: composes with any of the above; adds a leading-edge accent in primary or status colour.

Apply one style per region. Mixing Elevated and Outlined cards in the same grid creates competing depth cues. Pick the style that matches the region's elevation budget: content surfaces use Elevated; dashboards and admin tooling use Outlined or Filled.

Variant

Elevated · default

The default style. A subtle shadow plus a 1px border separates the card from the canvas. Reach for Elevated on content blocks, intake forms, and feed items where lift is the cue that the card belongs above the page.

02 / Card — Glass

Glass.

Glass is the only surface treatment the Card component ships. Every style variant in section 02 is glass at a different density; this section demonstrates the treatment over visual content, where the blur becomes the dominant cue. On the flat page canvas glass reads as a near-solid panel — that's the intended low-noise behaviour, not a missing effect.

Default · .card--glass.

The light-frost glass card with real content. Backdrop tint, blur, border, outer shadow, and the inner rim highlight resolve to the system glass rail in tokens.css. Sat over imagery so the blur reads.

Compositional overrides. Two modifiers exist for cases the variant identity doesn't cover. .card--glass-heavy swaps the default light frost for a near-opaque slab — modal sheets and drawer chrome where the surface needs to dominate the backdrop. .card--glass-tinted wires the glass through the primary ramp instead of white — brand-skinned overlays and promotional surfaces. Both compose with any style variant.

Interactive states tint up, never flip opaque. Hover and pressed shift the glass to a higher tint; focus draws the system ring; disabled drops opacity. Blur and translucency persist across every state — the glass identity is preserved end to end.

03 / Card — Versions

Versions.

Surface adaptations beyond the state matrix. Default: light canvas (rest). Inverted: re-wires every colour role for dark or brand backdrops. is-selected: state modifier marking the active card in a navigation list with a primary-tinted background, primary border, and primary ring shadow.

Default · Inverted · Selected applied to elevated

Default · light surface.

The card on the canvas at rest. Every other version is a deliberate move away from this baseline; if a region doesn't need a dark or selected version, it should sit here.
