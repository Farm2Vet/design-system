III-05 / Components Card.

A bounded container that groups related content. Composed from a slot-based anatomy and modified through style, layout, functional, and surface variants. All declarations resolve through the --card-* token layer.

Architectural note · scope boundary

FAQ / Q&A patterns are documented under Accordion, not Card. A disclosure that expands to reveal hidden content has its own keyboard contract, animation timing, and aria semantics. Card therefore does not ship a .card--question variant; the equivalent affordance lives under component-accordion.html.

Cards are glanceable surfaces. A card surfaces the most important fact first and pushes detail to a destination. If a reader has to study a card to extract its meaning, the card is doing too much.

01 / Card — Anatomy

Anatomy.

Slot-based composition. Every variant exposes a subset of the slots below; sub-components (badges, prices, ratings, metrics, avatars) attach into any slot that accepts content.

Slot order is hierarchy. The stack reads eyebrow → title → subtitle → body → footer in that order because each slot ranks lower than the one above it. The title is the only required slot — it carries the card's primary signal. Optional slots earn their place by adding signal, not decoration.

02 / Card — Style variants

Elevation.

Modifiers that control the card's surface treatment. Elevated: shadow plus border (default). Filled: tinted background, no shadow. Outlined: thicker border, no shadow. Left-bar: composes with any of the above; adds a leading-edge accent in primary or status colour.

Apply one style per region. Mixing Elevated and Outlined cards in the same grid creates competing depth cues. Pick the style that matches the region's elevation budget: content surfaces use Elevated; dashboards and admin tooling use Outlined or Filled.

Elevated · default.

The default style. A subtle shadow plus a 1px border separates the card from the canvas. Reach for Elevated on content blocks, intake forms, and feed items where lift is the cue that the card belongs above the page.

03 / Card — Layout & orientation

Orientation.

Modifiers that control how content arranges within the card. Vertical: media on top, content below (default). Horizontal: media on the leading edge. Content-only: no media slot. List: rows bleeding to the card's edges.

Vertical · Content-only · List grid layouts

Vertical · standard.

Media on top, content beneath. The canonical browse-feed and gallery shape — use it for blog/article cards, product cards, marketing grids, anywhere the image leads the scan.

04 / Card — Functional variants

Modifiers.

Modifiers that add behaviour to a card. Compose with style and layout variants.

Guideline · one level of interactivity

A card has at most one interactive surface. If the card contains its own buttons or links (.card__actions, links inside the body, etc.), the card itself must NOT also be made interactive — no .is-interactive, no <a class="card"> wrapper, no hover/pressed state on the surface. Nested click targets create ambiguous affordances and break keyboard order. Choose: either the surface is the affordance, or the inner controls are.

One tap, one outcome. When a card carries a CTA, that CTA should complete the user's intent or hand off cleanly to a destination. A card is a glanceable surface, not the first step of a multi-modal flow — if a single tap on the card opens a modal that opens another modal, the work belongs on a detail page instead.

Interactive · whole-card link.

A card whose entire surface is the affordance. Wrap with an <a> or <button>, or apply .is-interactive, to enable hover, focus, and pressed states. Use it for feed cards, navigation tiles, and dashboard shortcuts — anywhere a single primary action is the whole point.

Wrap any card with an <a> or <button> — or apply .is-interactive — to enable hover/focus/pressed states.

States. Default → hover (lift + shadow) → pressed (settle) → focus (ring); disabled supported. Interactions. Single primary action only; avoid nested clickable elements that aren't buttons. Responsive. Hover is a desktop affordance — on touch, the pressed state should still register tactile feedback.

A card that toggles between selected and unselected. Use it for multi-select grids (species, symptoms, treatments), content pickers, and bulk-operation surfaces. Multi-select is the default; single-select wires up via JS by clearing other .is-selected on click.

05 / Card — Content patterns

Patterns.

Recipes — compositions of existing slots and sub-components. Not new variants. Four canonical shapes: Product, Profile, KPI, Article.

One card, one job. A recipe is the shape a card takes when it carries a single kind of payload — a product, a person, a metric, an article. If a card seems to need two of these at once, split it into two cards or move secondary content into a detail view.

Product · commerce.

A recipe composing media, badges, title, rating, price, and footer actions. The surface is non-interactive because the footer carries explicit CTAs. Use it in catalog grids, search results, and recommendation rails.

06 / Card — Glass scale

Glass.

Translucent surface modifiers with backdrop blur. Five tiers ordered by frost density: Clear, Light (default), Frost, Heavy, Tinted. Each tier pairs a blur value with a tint opacity — higher blur correlates with higher opacity to keep content legible.

Tier selection. Pick by the visual density of what sits behind the card. .card--glass-clear: floating overlays. .card--glass: callouts on subtle backdrops. .card--glass-frost: content surfaces over busy imagery. .card--glass-heavy: modal-grade sheets. .card--glass-tinted: when the brand should colour the glass itself.

Interactive states are preserved. Every tier's hover/pressed states tint up rather than flipping to an opaque surface. Blur and translucency persist across all states.

Clear · barely there.

The lightest tier — a whisper of blur, around 25% white tint. Use it for floating chips and toolbars over imagery that should remain the dominant visual element.

Tier 01 · clear

Lightest tier — floating chips and toolbars over imagery that should remain dominant.

Pairs --blur-sm with the lowest tint in the scale. Hover and pressed tint up rather than flipping to opaque, so the glass effect persists across every state.

Light · default tier.

The system default — visible blur without dominating, so the canvas still informs the surface. Reach for .card--glass when you need glass on a subtle backdrop and don't have a reason to step up or down the scale.

Tier 02 · light

Balanced frost.

--blur-lg · light tint

The system default. Visible blur without dominating; the canvas still informs the surface.

Uses --blur-lg at a balanced tint. This is the baseline — every other tier is a deliberate move away from this default.

Frost · content-grade.

A content-grade tier for surfaces that need to dampen a complex backdrop while still feeling translucent. Around 75% white tint paired with --blur-xl.
