Vaccines

Vietnam has commercialized African swine fever (ASF) vaccines (NAVET-ASFVAC, AVAC ASF LIVE). These are live attenuated vaccines.

Mechanism & Risks: Live attenuated vaccines carry risks of vaccine virus shedding into the environment or reversion to virulence if biosafety management and vaccination practices are inadequate.

Recommendations:

  • Use only in fattening pigs. Extreme caution is required, or vaccination should be avoided, in breeding sows (unless the latest official guidance from the Department of Animal Health confirms safety for pregnancy), due to the risk of embryonic/fetal effects and vertical transmission.
  • Vaccinate pigs at 4–8 weeks of age.
  • Closely monitor post-vaccination reactions within the first 72 hours.

Treatment

No effective treatment exists; antibiotics and antipyretics are ineffective. Recommended actions include immediate reporting to veterinary authorities, culling of the herd, and implementing strict biosecurity and disinfection protocols.

Prevention

Currently, there is no treatment for this disease. In this case, the recommendations include:

Immediate Disease Control

  • Immediately isolate all affected pigs.
  • Stop all movement of pigs in and out of the farm.
  • Do not provide further treatment until there is intervention from the veterinary authorities.
  • Closely monitor for new cases with symptoms such as fever, loss of appetite, fatigue, skin discoloration, or diarrhea.

Notify Local Veterinary Authorities

  • In Vietnam, it is necessary to contact the local Sub-Department of Animal Health or the Sub-Department of Livestock Production and Animal Health.
  • African Swine Fever is a notifiable disease under Vietnamese veterinary law.

Send Samples for ASF Testing

  • ASF testing should be conducted when pigs show clinical signs such as fever.
  • Collect inguinal lymph node samples for testing and avoid performing necropsies to prevent environmental contamination with the virus.

What Not to Do

  • Do not sell, slaughter, or bury pigs without government permission.
  • Do not disinfect the farm immediately, as this may interfere with sample collection for testing.

Handling Based on the Severity of the Outbreak

  • Very severe outbreak: The optimal measure is to depopulate the entire herd to prevent the risk of spread. At the same time, it is necessary to clean and disinfect the entire farming area and its surroundings.
  • Severe outbreak but not yet spread throughout the entire herd: It is necessary to cull problematic groups and high-risk individuals. Strictly control the spread, handle empty pens and related areas. Restocking is only permitted with control and risk assessment.
  • Less severe cases: More flexible handling is possible by quickly culling sick or at-risk groups of pigs. Strict spread control and farm hygiene must still be maintained. Restocking should only be carried out after biosecurity has been confirmed.

ASF Prevention and Control Procedures

A comprehensive and strict ASF prevention and control procedure should be applied, including:

  • Human control: Restrict farm access, implement disinfection, and change protective clothing.
  • Insect and rodent control: Eliminate intermediate hosts that can carry the pathogen.
  • Tool and equipment control: Ensure all tools are disinfected.
  • Pig flow control: Strictly manage movement to avoid cross-contamination.
  • Handling and disposal of problematic pigs: Includes steps for culling, isolation, destruction, and testing.
  • Farm hygiene: Regular and thorough disinfection after culling the infected herd.
  • Controlled restocking: Only proceed after the facility meets biosecurity standards and with the supervision of veterinary authorities.

Overview

The most common and typical form. Mortality is very high, occurring within 7 days of the appearance of clinical signs.

Disease rates

Mortality 0–100%

The mortality rate is very high, ranging from 0% to 100% depending on the virulence of the virus:

  • For highly virulent isolates, the mortality rate is usually 90 - 100%
  • For moderately virulent isolates, the mortality rate is approximately 20 - 40% in adult pigs and 70 - 80% in young pigs
  • For low virulence isolates, the mortality rate is approximately 10 - 30%

Morbidity 40–85%

The morbidity rate ranges from 40% to 85%, depending on the virulence of the virus isolate and the route of infection. In acute outbreaks, the morbidity rate can reach 100%

Additional

Immediate Actions

Immediate actions

In the event of suspected African Swine Fever (ASF), Classical Swine Fever (CSF), or other diseases for which treatment is prohibited, the following actions are mandatory:

1. Immediate Disease Control Measures

  • Isolate all affected pigs immediately.
  • Halt all animal movement on and off the farm.
  • DO NOT treat the pigs further until the official veterinary authority arrives.
  • Monitor new cases closely for clinical signs (fever, off-feed/anorexia, lethargy, skin discoloration, diarrhea).

2. Official Notification

  • In Vietnam, contact the Sub-Department of Animal Health (Chi cục Thú y) or the Sub-Department of Livestock and Animal Health (Chi cục Chăn nuôi và Thú y) in your area.
  • African Swine Fever is a mandatory reportable disease under Vietnamese animal health law.

3. Sample Submission for Testing

  • Samples should be collected when pigs show clinical signs (e.g., fever).
  • For testing. Do not perform full necropsies as this may spread contamination.

Sampling Targets

  1. Pigs suspected of ASF: off-feed, sick, fever >40°C, or dead.
  2. F1, F2 generation pigs from suspected animals (collect 5–10 samples).
  3. Blood samples for ASF surveillance.

Note on Testing

  • Samples can be pooled (maximum 5 samples per mix) for RT-PCR ASF testing.
  • Send samples to at least two laboratories for cross-validation.

Prohibited Actions (Do Nots)

  • Do not sell, slaughter, or bury pigs without government authorization.
  • Do not immediately disinfect the housing yourself — this can interfere with sample collection for testing.

If ASF is Confirmed

  • All pigs on the farm may be culled according to national policy.
  • The farm will be disinfected under official supervision.
  • Enhanced biosecurity and movement restrictions will be implemented to contain the outbreak.

ASF Sudden, high Early transient Cyanosis, rapid spread High suspicion due to lesions, no vaccine. No blood from nose/mouth; no burnt smell in pen; uncommon to see hind limb paralysis.

Actinobacillus pleuropneumoniae (APP) Respiratory distress, hemorrhagic lungs Yes Hemorrhagic cough, dog-sitting, rapid spread Would expect more respiratory signs and fever.
PRRS Variable Yes Variable respiratory signs, eye swelling, wasting Usually with fever, diarrhea, reproductive signs (abortions), cyanosis.
Swine Erysipela Sudden death Yes Cyanosis, skin lesions, anorexia Usually slower spread; lesions often diamond-shaped.
Streptococcus suis Sudden death. Mortality can reach up to 20% without prophylactic treatment. Variable Cyanosis, limb paralysis, purple skin spots, arthritis, meningitis, eye inflammation Usually causes meningitis, not footpad bruising & neurologic signs, edema. May be early skin vasculitis or mild septicemia signs from S. suis. Subacute cases may not always show high fever. Reduced appetite common in early illness. S. suis is the most important bacterial pathogen in postweaned piglets and is an emerging zoonotic agent. Clinical signs of infection are observed mainly in weaned pigs (2–5 weeks after weaning), rarely in suckling and growing pigs, and almost never in adult animals.
