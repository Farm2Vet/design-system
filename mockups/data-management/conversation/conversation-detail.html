What to do when my herd of piglets have diarrhea with yellow feces, lethargy, losing weight rapidly, and a few piglets are vomiting?

Conversation transcript
Turn 01 Producer
What to do when my herd of piglets have diarrhea with yellow feces, lethargy, losing weight rapidly, and a few piglets are vomiting.
Turn 02 Veterinarian
Diarrhea in piglets can be caused by a number of factors. To get the most accurate diagnosis, please share how old your piglets are and roughly how much they weigh.
Turn 03 Producer
My piglets are 3–5 days old.
Turn 04 Veterinarian
Can you tell me how many piglets out of the litter are affected?
Turn 05 Producer
8 out of 12 piglets are affected.
Turn 06 Veterinarian
Please describe the symptoms you've observed — the condition of the feces, how rapidly they're losing weight, whether they're vomiting, and whether they lie close to the sow.
Turn 07 Producer
They have yellow diarrhea, lethargy, rapid weight loss, and a few are vomiting.
Turn 08 Veterinarian
Have you tried any treatment so far?
Turn 09 Producer
I've tried various antibiotics, but there's no improvement. I'm also not sure about the right dose or type.
Turn 10 Veterinarian
Can you share the farm's vaccination history — especially against E. coli?
Turn 11 Producer
The E. coli vaccine was administered to the farrowing sow.
Turn 12 Veterinarian

Based on your description, the piglets are most likely suffering from Transmissible Gastroenteritis (TGE) — a coronavirus. Recommended actions:

Supportive care. Provide oral electrolytes and glucose solutions to affected piglets to combat dehydration.

Biosecurity. Isolate affected piglets. Disinfect pens with virucidal agents.

Veterinary care. Consult an on-site veterinarian for confirmation by PCR and a tailored treatment plan.

Supporting points
  1. Age (3–5 days) and morbidity (8/12) strongly suggest a transmissible neonatal enteric pathogen.
  2. Sow has been vaccinated against E. coli — reducing the probability of colibacillosis.
  3. Yellow watery diarrhea + vomiting + rapid weight loss is a textbook TGE presentation.
  4. Antibiotics alone won't resolve a viral etiology — supportive care is the first-line response.
