Architectural note · scope boundary

FAQ / Q&A patterns are documented under Accordion, not Card. A disclosure that expands to reveal hidden content has its own keyboard contract, animation timing, and aria semantics. 02 / Card — Style variants

Style by elevation.

Modifiers that control the card's surface treatment. Elevated: shadow plus border (default). Filled: tinted background, no shadow. Outlined: thicker border, no shadow. Left-bar: composes with any of the above; adds a leading-edge accent in primary or status colour.

Apply one style per region. Mixing Elevated and Outlined cards in the same grid creates competing depth cues. Pick the style that matches the region's elevation budget: content surfaces use Elevated; dashboards and admin tooling use Outlined or Filled.

04 / Card — Functional variants

Behaviour modifiers.

Modifiers that add behaviour to a card. Compose with style and layout variants.

Guideline · one level of interactivity

A card has at most one interactive surface. If the card contains its own buttons or links (.card__actions, links inside the body, etc.), the card itself must NOT also be made interactive — no .is-interactive, no <a class="card"> wrapper, no hover/pressed state on the surface. Nested click targets create ambiguous affordances and break keyboard order. Choose: either the surface is the affordance, or the inner controls are.

06 / Card — Glass scale

Blur and transparency.

Translucent surface modifiers with backdrop blur. Five tiers ordered by frost density: Clear, Light (default), Frost, Heavy, Tinted. Each tier pairs a blur value with a tint opacity — higher blur correlates with higher opacity to keep content legible.

Tier selection. Pick by the visual density of what sits behind the card. .card--glass-clear: floating overlays. .card--glass: callouts on subtle backdrops. .card--glass-frost: content surfaces over busy imagery. .card--glass-heavy: modal-grade sheets. .card--glass-tinted: when the brand should colour the glass itself.

Interactive states are preserved. Every tier's hover/pressed states tint up rather than flipping to an opaque surface. Blur and translucency persist across all states.

07 / Card — States

Five interaction states.

Five interaction states applied automatically when the card is the affordance — i.e. an <a>, a <button>, or carries .is-interactive. Pressed releases a primary-tinted aura that mirrors the swatch-pulse behaviour on the primitives page. Cards with internal buttons or links must NOT carry these states — see the interactivity guideline in section 04.
