01 / Background

Background color.

Default

Primary

Inverted

Use sparingly. Inverted surfaces are for anchoring a single moment of contrast — a hero band, a feature callout, or a footer. Limit to one or two regions per view. Broad use flattens hierarchy and competes with primary action surfaces for attention.

02 / Text

Text color.

Default

Inverted

Brand and emphasis

Accent is reserved for CTAs. --text-accent and --text-accent-subtle are for primary call-to-action labels and key action language only — not for body copy, headings, or decorative emphasis. For brand voice within content, use --text-primary or --text-emphasis instead.

07 / Gradient

Brand gradients.

Multi-stop brand washes blending primary lime, secondary earth, and supporting tones across linear, vertical, and conic configurations.

Accent moments, not surfaces. Reserve gradients for hero areas, brand marks, and feature callouts where the brand should lead the eye — typically once per view, never more than twice. Avoid as default component backgrounds, dense content surfaces, or large layout regions, where they fight legibility and dilute hierarchy. Pair every gradient with neutral surfaces around it so the wash remains the focal point.
